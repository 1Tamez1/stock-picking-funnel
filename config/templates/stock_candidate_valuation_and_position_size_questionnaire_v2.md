# Stock Candidate Valuation And Position Size Questionnaire v2

Purpose: decide whether a fully underwritten stock deserves entry into **Execution Rules**, belongs on a watchlist with a defined price or fact trigger, should return to underwriting because a key valuation input is still unreliable, or should be archived.

This is the fifth stage of the funnel:

1. Screening
2. Business Underwriting
3. Management Underwriting
4. Financial Underwriting
5. Valuation and Position Size
6. Execution Rules

This document is only for **Valuation and Position Size**. It assumes the business, management, and financial work is already far enough along to support a valuation range. It is not a screening memo, not a substitute for underwriting, and not an execution plan. Its job is to convert the prior stages into a conservative value range, a clear price ladder, an expected-return view, and a justified position size.

## Core Rules

- **Approve For Execution** means "the stock is cheap enough and sized appropriately to move into Execution Rules," not "buy at any price."
- **Watchlist** means "the business may qualify, but price, evidence, or portfolio conditions are not yet good enough."
- **Return To Underwriting** means "valuation depends on unresolved Business, Management, or Financial questions."
- **Archive** means "do not spend more time unless price, facts, or business quality materially change."
- Start from the **Financial Underwriting handoff**, not from a blank sheet.
- Do not re-answer stage-1, stage-2, stage-3, or stage-4 questions unless valuation work materially changes the answer.
- Stage 5 answers valuation questions only: method choice, value range, price ladder, expected return, downside, and position size.
- Import normalized economics from Financial Underwriting. Do not rebuild them here unless a valuation method requires a clearly stated adjustment.
- Use ranges, not single-point precision. Intrinsic value is an estimate, not a fact.
- Keep enterprise value, equity value, and per-share value distinct. Do not mix them casually.
- The wider the uncertainty, the larger the required discount and the smaller the position.
- Risk is the chance of permanent capital loss or serious opportunity cost, not short-term price volatility by itself.
- A lower-quality, cyclical, leveraged, opaque, or capital-hungry business requires both a lower entry price and a smaller size.
- Position size is earned by knowability, downside containment, balance-sheet strength, capital-allocation proof, liquidity, and opportunity cost.
- Large size should be rare. Bet heavily only when the odds are clearly in your favor.
- Never size from upside alone. Size from downside, probability of being wrong, and consequences if wrong.
- Relative valuation is normally a **cross-check**, not the primary method, unless the situation is explicitly relative by nature.
- If expected return without multiple expansion is inadequate, the burden of proof is very high.
- If a position can be justified only by an aggressive terminal multiple, a heroic growth path, or perfect execution, it is not ready.
- Stage 5 must answer these questions explicitly:
- What is the business worth conservatively?
- At what price does it become attractive?
- At what price is it clearly cheap enough to size up?
- At what price is it too expensive, even if the business is excellent?
- Execution Rules will decide how orders are placed and managed. This document should set valuation boundaries, not full trading behavior.
- The final output must state the exact next action in the funnel.

Target depth:

- **Archive / Return To Underwriting**: usually 30-90 minutes once prior underwriting exists.
- **Watchlist**: usually 1-2 hours.
- **Approve For Execution**: enough work to defend a conservative value range, a price ladder, downside, and position size; not enough to manage the trade day by day.

# Evidence, Sources, And Confidence

Use evidence grades beside important claims.

- **F** = audited, reported, or directly filed fact
- **O** = observable operating, customer, competitive, or industry evidence
- **M** = management claim
- **I** = analyst inference
- **V** = item to verify later

Use confidence tags beside important judgments.

- **High** = supported by several reliable facts or a long relevant record
- **Medium** = plausible, but one important item needs more evidence
- **Low** = early read, thin evidence, or material uncertainty

Rule: if approval or full-size position depends mainly on **M**, **I**, or **V**, the default action is a smaller position, Watchlist, or Return To Underwriting.

Minimum source expectations for **Approve For Execution**:

- Completed or clearly sufficient Business Underwriting conclusion
- Completed or clearly sufficient Management Underwriting conclusion
- Completed or clearly sufficient Financial Underwriting conclusion
- Latest annual report or 10-K
- Latest quarterly report or 10-Q
- Share count, buyback, dilution, and capital-allocation history
- Debt maturities, liquidity, and off-balance-sheet obligations review
- At least one conservative primary valuation method appropriate to the business type
- At least one relevant cross-check
- Explicit downside case and opportunity-cost comparison

If these are not reviewed, Approve For Execution requires an explicit reason.

Choose the method to fit the business. Do not force the business into the method you happen to like.

Method guide:

| Situation | Usually appropriate primary anchor | Usually required cross-check | Usually inappropriate as primary |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |

# Part X. Business Category And Valuation Standard

## Category Options

Choose the best fit and then apply the matching valuation discipline.

### Exceptional Compounder

- Needs unusually high durability, reinvestment quality, and per-share value creation.
- Can justify a smaller discount than a normal business, but still needs a clear too-expensive line.
- Full size normally requires both strong knowability and a clear no-rerating return.

### Good Predictable Business

- Starter size can be justified at a visible but not extreme discount.
- Larger size requires a clearer gap to conservative worth and strong balance-sheet endurance.
- Do not let quality become an excuse to blur the attractive line and the too-expensive line.

### Cyclical / Commodity / Asset-Heavy Business

- Value on normalized earnings power, trough-aware economics, or asset value, not peak earnings.
- Needs a strong balance sheet and a large enough discount to survive timing error.
- Size up only when both the price and the cycle position are favorable.

### Financial / Insurer

- Use book-value, tangible-book, reserve, spread, capital, or distributable-earnings logic that matches the institution.
- Do not force industrial metrics where they do not belong.
- Demand wider caution where leverage, reserves, or asset quality make downside harder to see.

### Special Situation

- Value on the actual event path, terms, and downside protection.
- Size only if the path to realization, timeline, and failure cost are clear.
- Do not hide event uncertainty inside a normal-business valuation template.

### Too Hard

- If value depends on assumptions that are too fragile, circular, or opaque, stop here.
- A company can be excellent and still be too hard to value responsibly.

**Business Category Result**: Exceptional Compounder / Good Predictable / Cyclical-Commodity / Financial-Insurer / Special Situation / Too Hard
