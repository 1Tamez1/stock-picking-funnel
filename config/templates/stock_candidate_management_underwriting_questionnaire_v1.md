# Stock Candidate Management Underwriting Questionnaire v1

Purpose: decide whether management deserves entry into **Financial Underwriting**, belongs on a watchlist with a defined trigger, or should be archived.

This is the third stage of the funnel:

1. Screening
2. Business Underwriting
3. Management Underwriting
4. Financial Underwriting
5. Valuation and Position Size
6. Execution Rules

This document is only for **Management Underwriting**. It is not a business memo, not a full financial memo, not a valuation memo, and not a buy decision. Its job is to judge whether the people allocating capital and representing owners are rational, candid, disciplined, and trustworthy enough to deserve the next funnel stage.

This document is designed to be used **after Business Underwriting**, not instead of Business Underwriting. It should deepen the human, incentive, governance, and capital-allocation questions left open by earlier stages. It should not repeat market boundary, moat mechanism, unit economics, or full financial normalization unless management behavior materially changes the answer.

## Core Rules

- **Pass Management Underwriting** means "open Financial Underwriting," not "buy."
- **Watchlist** means "potentially investable, but a specific management fact, action, or change is missing."
- **Archive** means "stop unless management behavior, incentives, governance, or stewardship materially changes."
- Start from the **Business Underwriting handoff**, not from a blank sheet.
- Do not re-answer stage-1 or stage-2 business questions unless management behavior invalidates the earlier answer.
- Judge management on decisions made under discretion: capital allocation, disclosure, compensation, governance, hiring, firing, decentralization, and crisis response.
- Character outranks charm, narrative skill, and short-term EPS.
- Start with behavior, not biography. Favor what management has done over what management says.
- Judge management by per-share value creation, not company size, reported EPS optics, or deal volume.
- Buybacks are good only when price and value make them good.
- Stock-based compensation is a real cost, not a harmless add-back.
- Candor is tested in bad periods, not good periods.
- A strong business can still be a bad investment if managers misuse capital.
- Boards are judged by behavior, not by the word "independent" in a proxy.
- If you would not trust this team with your whole net worth in a private company, do not trust them with a public one.
- Do not use a single score. Some failures are disqualifying regardless of strengths elsewhere.
- Preserve downstream questions for later stages; do not let Financial Underwriting or Valuation solve a management problem that belongs here.
- The final output must state the next action in the funnel.

Target depth:

- **Archive**: often 45-90 minutes is enough if the problem is obvious.
- **Watchlist**: usually 2-4 hours with proxy, letters, calls, capital-allocation casework, and external management evidence.
- **Pass Management Underwriting**: enough work to justify Financial Underwriting, not enough to size a position.

# Evidence, Sources, And Confidence

Use the same evidence grades and confidence tags as the Screening and Business Underwriting questionnaires.

Use evidence grades beside important claims.

- **F** = filed, reported, or directly disclosed fact
- **O** = observable behavior, operating action, or externally checkable evidence
- **M** = management claim
- **I** = analyst inference
- **V** = item to verify later

Use confidence tags beside important judgments.

- **High** = supported by several reliable facts or a long relevant record
- **Medium** = plausible, but one important item needs more evidence
- **Low** = early read, thin evidence, or material uncertainty

Rule: if a management decision depends mainly on **M**, **I**, or **V**, the stock normally belongs on the Watchlist, not Pass Management Underwriting.

Minimum source expectations for **Pass Management Underwriting**:

- Business Underwriting document and handoff reviewed
- Latest proxy or remuneration filing
- Latest annual report or 10-K
- At least three years of shareholder letters or equivalent management communications
- At least four earnings calls, including at least one difficult period if available
- Major buyback, issuance, dividend, debt, acquisition, and divestiture actions reviewed against contemporaneous management language
- Insider ownership and insider transaction review
- Board, related-party, control, and governance review
- Some external management evidence beyond company-prepared language when the management claim matters

If these are not reviewed, Pass Management Underwriting requires an explicit reason.

# Part IV. Hard Management Gates

Each hard gate should result in **Pass**, **Watchlist**, **Archive**. A Watchlist result is acceptable only if the uncertainty is specific and verifiable.

## 1. Integrity, Candor, And Accessibility

Questions:

- Do letters and calls discuss mistakes and bad news plainly?
- Does management talk freely when things are going badly, or does it clam up?
- Are metrics consistent, or does management shift goalposts?
- Is there promotional language, blame shifting, or guidance theater?
- Are adjusted metrics, KPIs, or footnotes hiding economic reality?
- When asked a hard question, does management answer it, dodge it, or delegate it away?

Rule:

- **Pass** if management reports as if owners are partners and no material credibility issue remains.
- **Watchlist** if candor is mixed but the problem is narrow, nonfatal, and verifiable.
- **Archive** if management is materially promotional, evasive, inaccessible, or misleading.

**Result**: Pass / Watchlist / Archive

**Notes**:

## 2. Rational Capital Allocation And Countercyclicality

Questions:

- Does management explain reinvestment, dividends, buybacks, debt, acquisitions, and cash balances as opportunity-cost decisions?
- Has management likely created at least one dollar of per-share value per dollar retained over time?
- When internal reinvestment returns are low, does capital get redirected rationally?
- Is management willing to do nothing when no high-return use exists?
- Has management acted countercyclically when others were procyclical?
- Does management sacrifice short-term EPS optics when long-term owner value requires it?

Rule:

- **Pass** if capital allocation appears rational, owner-oriented, and grounded in per-share value.
- **Watchlist** if the record is mixed but the uncertainty is specific and testable.
- **Archive** if retained capital is habitually misused, procyclically squandered, or defended with weak logic.

**Result**: Pass / Watchlist / Archive

**Notes**:

## 3. Buybacks, Issuance, And Dilution Discipline

Questions:

- Have buybacks been concentrated when shares were likely below value?
- Have repurchases merely offset stock-based compensation or acquisition dilution?
- Has stock been issued only when the company received at least as much business value as it gave?
- Do management comments focus on per-share intrinsic value rather than EPS optics?
- Are options, convertibles, and share issuance treated like selling part of the business?

Rule:

- **Pass** if buybacks and issuance likely increased per-share value or at least did not materially destroy it.
- **Watchlist** if the record is uncertain and needs price/value or dilution work.
- **Archive** if management routinely repurchases overpriced stock, issues undervalued stock, or hides dilution behind adjusted metrics.

**Result**: Pass / Watchlist / Archive

**Notes**:

## 4. Incentives, Compensation, And Agency

Questions:

- Are bonus and equity metrics tied to per-share value, return on capital, owner earnings, or other useful long-term measures?
- Are metrics easy to game through leverage, acquisitions, buybacks, or accounting?
- Are incentives measured over a long enough period to discourage short-termism?
- Is management ownership meaningful and preferably built with purchased shares rather than only granted stock?
- Is stock-based compensation a tool, or a permanent transfer from owners to employees?
- Are downside, clawback, or deferral features real enough to matter?

Rule:

- **Pass** if incentives point broadly toward long-term owner value and management has meaningful owner exposure.
- **Watchlist** if compensation is imperfect but the problem is narrow and fixable.
- **Archive** if incentives materially reward size, optics, leverage, or dilution at owner expense.

**Result**: Pass / Watchlist / Archive

**Notes**:

## 5. Governance, Control, And Minority-Holder Treatment

Questions:

- Are directors meaningfully independent in behavior, not merely on paper?
- If the company is family-controlled, founder-controlled, state-influenced, or dual-class, have minority owners historically been treated fairly?
- Are related-party transactions, unequal treatment, takeover defenses, or control features owner-friendly or owner-hostile?
- Does the board challenge management on pay, deals, leverage, and succession?
- Are owners treated as partners or as counterparties to be managed?

Rule:

- **Pass** if governance appears owner-protective and no material abuse is visible.
- **Watchlist** if governance is imperfect but the risk is bounded and specific.
- **Archive** if owners are materially disadvantaged by governance structure, controller behavior, or board passivity.

**Result**: Pass / Watchlist / Archive

**Notes**:

## 6. Management Depth, Delegation, And Culture

Questions:

- Is there credible CEO and CFO succession?
- Is authority delegated intelligently, or hoarded at the top?
- Are promising younger executives being developed in real depth?
- Does management welcome contrary suggestions and bad news from below?
- Would culture, capital allocation, and reporting standards likely survive key-person loss?
- Are strong operators empowered, or does everything depend on one hero?

Rule:

- **Pass** if key-person risk is acceptable, authority is delegated sensibly, and the management system looks durable.
- **Watchlist** if depth is unclear but the issue is temporary or learnable.
- **Archive** if the investment case depends on a single irreplaceable person, weak delegation, or a culture too fragile to survive strain.

**Result**: Pass / Watchlist / Archive

**Notes**:

## 7. Resistance To The Institutional Imperative And Empire Building

Questions:

- Does management copy peers on leverage, acquisitions, guidance, or compensation, or act independently?
- Has the company ever shrunk, paused growth, exited a bad business, or returned cash when the crowd was still expanding?
- Are banker, consultant, or board incentives obviously steering decisions?
- Does management prefer activity over rational inactivity?
- Is management trying to build value, or trying to build a larger empire?

Rule:

- **Pass** if management shows independent judgment and can resist fashionable but value-destructive behavior.
- **Watchlist** if the team may be rational but needs one or two behavior checks.
- **Archive** if lemming-like behavior or size worship clearly dominates decision making.

**Result**: Pass / Watchlist / Archive

**Notes**:

# Part X. Management Category And Next Underwriting Standard

Pick the category based on behavior, not presentation skill. Do not use a criteria count mechanically.

## Category Options

### Exceptional Owner-Operators

Traits:

- High integrity
- Strong owner orientation
- Excellent capital allocation record
- Sensible buybacks and issuance
- Candid communication
- Real independence of thought
- Thoughtful succession and low bureaucracy

Management standard:

- Can pass with smaller imperfections only if the record is long and governance is clean.

Financial Underwriting must prove next:

- Reported returns are real.
- Owner earnings are clean.
- No hidden liabilities or accounting offsets the management quality.

### Rational Stewards

Traits:

- Capable operators
- Sensible if not exceptional capital allocation
- Adequate candor
- Aligned enough incentives
- Acceptable governance
- Some owner orientation

Management standard:

- Can pass if no fatal misalignment remains and the per-share record is acceptable.

Financial Underwriting must prove next:

- Retained capital economics are real.
- Cash conversion supports reported quality.
- Leverage and stock-based compensation do not erode owner returns.

### Capable But Agency-Risky

Traits:

- Decent operators
- Mixed capital allocation
- Material compensation, buyback, issuance, or acquisition concerns
- Governance may be serviceable but not protective

Management standard:

- Usually Watchlist unless the misalignment is narrow, verifiable, and fixable.

Financial Underwriting must prove next:

- Whether the economics are strong enough to offset the management discount.
- Whether dilution, leverage, or acquisition accounting hides weak decisions.

### Promotional / Empire-Building

Traits:

- Narrative-heavy communication
- Deal activity favored over value
- Peer imitation
- EPS optics over per-share value
- Large pay with weak owner outcomes

Management standard:

- Archive unless there is clear and already visible change.

Next step:

- Do not proceed in the normal funnel without concrete evidence of a new capital allocation regime.

### Control / Governance Risk

Traits:

- Related-party issues
- Unequal treatment
- Weak board challenge
- Entrenchment
- Opaque decision making

Management standard:

- Archive unless governance risk is both narrow and remediable.

Next step:

- Use a governance-risk or special-situation process only if the discount is extreme and the risk is clearly bounded.

### Too Hard
