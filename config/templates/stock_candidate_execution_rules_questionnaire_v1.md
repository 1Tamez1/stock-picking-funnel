# Stock Candidate Execution Rules Questionnaire v1

Purpose: decide whether a fully underwritten and valued stock should be bought now, entered through staged orders, held without fresh buying, moved to a watchlist with a defined price or fact trigger, returned to underwriting because the facts changed, trimmed, exited, or archived.

This is the sixth stage of the funnel:

1. Screening
2. Business Underwriting
3. Management Underwriting
4. Financial Underwriting
5. Valuation and Position Size
6. Execution Rules

This document is only for **Execution Rules**. It assumes the business, management, financial, and valuation work is already far enough along to support action. It is not a substitute for underwriting, not a substitute for valuation, and not a license to react to short-term noise. Its job is to convert the prior stages into a stand-alone company snapshot plus exact rules for entry, adds, holds, trims, exits, and return-to-underwriting triggers.

This document is designed to be used **after Valuation and Position Size**, not instead of it. It should not rebuild the thesis unless price, facts, or portfolio conditions materially change. It should also be usable later as the position-operating brief for an existing holding.

## Core Rules

- **Execute Starter Now** means "buy within the approved valuation range and size discipline," not "buy because the stock moved."
- **Enter Staged Orders** means "the thesis is actionable, but liquidity, price ladder, or uncertainty requires a tranche plan."
- **Hold Existing** means "the business is still ownable, but the current price, sizing, or portfolio context does not justify fresh buying."
- **Trim** means "reduce because weight, valuation, or opportunity cost is no longer acceptable."
- **Exit** means "sell because the thesis broke, the trust broke, the accounting broke, the balance sheet broke, or the opportunity is clearly inferior."
- **Watchlist** means "the company may still qualify, but price, evidence, liquidity, or portfolio conditions are not yet good enough."
- **Return To Underwriting** means "an important business, management, financial, or valuation assumption can no longer be trusted."
- **Archive** means "do not spend more time unless price, facts, or execution conditions materially change."
- Start from the **Valuation and Position Size handoff**, not from a blank sheet.
- Do not re-answer stage-1, stage-2, stage-3, stage-4, or stage-5 questions unless execution work materially changes the answer.
- Execution Rules answer action questions only: what to do now, at what prices, at what size, under what conditions, and what would invalidate the action.
- Price is an input, not thesis evidence.
- Do not buy above the approved **no-buy-above** line unless valuation is refreshed and explicitly changed.
- If price falls below the approved buy range because a fact worsened, do not average down automatically. First decide whether the lower price is a gift or a warning.
- No stop-losses based only on ordinary price volatility.
- Separate clearly: thesis break, valuation stretch, position-size breach, liquidity constraint, and tax friction. They are not the same problem.
- Use the correct listing, currency, and share count. Do not mix ADR, local line, or reporting currency casually.
- Refresh the quote with exact date, time, currency, and source before any execution decision.
- All buys require defined hold, trim, exit, and return-to-underwriting rules before the first share is bought.
- If a new filing, capital-allocation action, financing, management change, or operating update changes the facts materially, return to the correct stage before acting.
- Keep the rules simple enough to follow under stress.
- Execution should reduce unforced errors, not create more activity.

Target depth:

- **Archive / Return To Underwriting**: often 20-60 minutes once the earlier stages exist.
- **Watchlist**: usually 30-90 minutes.
- **Execute Starter Now / Enter Staged Orders / Hold Existing / Trim / Exit**: enough work to specify the company snapshot, price ladder, order construction, size discipline, monitoring triggers, and clear invalidation rules; not enough to become a live trading journal.

# Evidence, Sources, And Confidence

Use evidence grades beside important claims.

- **F** = audited, reported, or directly filed fact
- **O** = observable operating, customer, competitive, or industry evidence
- **M** = management claim
- **I** = analyst inference
- **V** = item to verify later

Use confidence tags beside important judgments.

- **High** = supported by several reliable facts or a long relevant record
- **Medium** = plausible, but one important item needs more evidence
- **Low** = early read, thin evidence, or material uncertainty

Rule: if an execution decision depends mainly on **M**, **I**, or **V**, the default action is smaller size, staged orders, Hold Existing, Watchlist, or Return To Underwriting rather than aggressive buying.

Minimum source expectations for **Execute Starter Now** or **Enter Staged Orders**:

- Completed or clearly sufficient Screening conclusion
- Completed or clearly sufficient Business Underwriting conclusion
- Completed or clearly sufficient Management Underwriting conclusion
- Completed or clearly sufficient Financial Underwriting conclusion
- Completed or clearly sufficient Valuation and Position Size conclusion
- Latest annual report or 10-K
- Latest quarterly report or 10-Q
- Current quote with exact date, time, currency, and source
- Current diluted share count, market cap, net debt or net cash, and enterprise value refreshed if price or capital structure changed materially
- Existing position, cost basis, available cash, and hard maximum position known
- Basic liquidity review: average daily value, spread, venue, and line selection
- Upcoming earnings date, filing date, and major expected corporate event checked
- Tax, account, mandate, and broker constraints reviewed if relevant

# Part X. Execution Pattern And Standard

Pick the category based on the actual setup, not on optimism.

## Category Options

### Immediate Starter

Traits:

- Current price is inside the approved starter range
- No major near-term fact makes the valuation stale
- Liquidity is manageable
- Starter size is justified, but full size is not required immediately
- Clear hold, add, trim, and exit rules already exist

Execution standard:

- Buy now only within the defined price, size, and order rules.

Monitoring standard:

- Review at the next defined event or price trigger, not because of ordinary noise.

### Staged Accumulation

Traits:

- Thesis is actionable
- Valuation is attractive enough to buy
- Liquidity, uncertainty, or position-size discipline requires several tranches
- The analyst wants time or evidence between tranches

Execution standard:

- Use a written tranche plan with maximum capital per tranche, timing rules, and invalidation rules.

Monitoring standard:

- Recheck facts between tranches. Do not complete the ladder automatically if new information changed the case.

### Hold Existing / No Fresh Buying

Traits:

- The company still appears ownable
- Existing position is acceptable
- Current price is not attractive enough for fresh buying, or opportunity cost is higher elsewhere
- Thesis is intact, but expected return from here is not strong enough for adds

Execution standard:

- Hold according to the defined review cadence. Do not add unless the price or facts improve enough to reopen buying.

Monitoring standard:

- Focus on thesis integrity, valuation drift, and position-size discipline.

### Watchlist Only

Traits:

- The company may still qualify later
- Price, liquidity, evidence, or opportunity cost is not yet good enough
- A specific trigger can be named

Execution standard:

- No order now. Write the trigger and review when it is hit.

Monitoring standard:

- Monitor only the stated trigger, not daily noise.

### Trim / Harvest

Traits:

- The position is too large, too expensive, or clearly inferior to another use of capital
- Thesis may still be intact
- The issue is mostly valuation, concentration, or opportunity cost

Execution standard:

- Trim according to the written size and valuation rules. Avoid improvisation.

Monitoring standard:

- Reassess whether further trimming or a full exit is needed after the first reduction.

### Exit / Broken Thesis

Traits:

- Business, management, financial integrity, or valuation foundation is broken
- Or the trust has broken badly enough that ownership is no longer acceptable
- Or the balance sheet and downside are no longer tolerable

Execution standard:

- Sell according to the written exit rule. Do not negotiate with a broken thesis.

Monitoring standard:

- Archive unless a clearly new case is built from the correct earlier stage.

### Too Hard To Execute

Traits:

- Listing, liquidity, tax, mandate, event, or knowledge constraints prevent sensible action
- The company may still be interesting, but execution quality is too poor

Execution standard:

- Do not force a trade.

Monitoring standard:

- Revisit only if the blocking execution constraint changes.

**Execution Pattern Result**: Immediate Starter / Staged Accumulation / Hold Existing / Watchlist Only / Trim-Harvest / Exit-Broken Thesis / Too Hard To Execute
