Snapshot notes:

- The SEP SIRVOES FAQ says the Reconocimiento de Validez Oficial de Estudios (RVOE) is the act by which the education authority recognizes a private institution's specific study plan and program within Mexico's national education system.
- The same FAQ says RVOE is granted program by program, so a private institution may have RVOE for some programs and not for others.
- The FAQ also says federal and state RVOE have the same validity within the national education system; the difference is the authority that grants them.
- UVM's website says its programs have official SEP validity, that it operates 22 campuses plus an online campus, and that it serves professional, executive, and postgraduate students across multiple modalities.

Why this matters for Business Underwriting:

- Mexico's private higher-education market has meaningful program-level regulatory requirements, which limits fly-by-night entry and makes compliance and program stewardship part of the competitive position.
- At the same time, RVOE works at the program level rather than granting blanket protection, so regulation supports incumbency but does not by itself prove a wide moat for Laureate's Mexico business.
