Snapshot notes:

- FY2021 release:
  - Continuing-operations revenue was about $1.09 billion and operating loss was about $4.6 million.
  - Cash was about $324.8 million and gross debt was about $157.3 million at 2021-12-31.
  - Management highlighted digital-capability investment and post-COVID recovery trends.
  - After the Walden sale, Laureate made two special cash distributions in late 2021 totaling roughly $1.4 billion and had fully repaid the senior notes.

- FY2022 release:
  - Revenue rose to about $1.24 billion and operating income to about $270.0 million.
  - Cash was about $85.2 million and gross debt about $234.2 million at 2022-12-31.
  - Laureate paid a special cash distribution of about $136.6 million in October 2022 and a special cash dividend of about $112 million in November 2022.
  - In November 2022, Laureate repurchased about 8.0 million shares for roughly $75 million in connection with a secondary offering.
  - Management also set medium-term targets including 30%+ adjusted EBITDA margin, capex below 5% of revenue, and about 50% adjusted EBITDA to unlevered free cash flow conversion.

- Q3 2023 release:
  - Management explicitly described Peru as having a more subdued backdrop because of economic softness while still calling Mexico robust.
  - Laureate announced a special cash dividend of $0.70 per share, about $110 million in aggregate.
  - Cash was about $130.9 million and gross debt about $134.7 million at 2023-09-30, so net debt was close to flat.

- FY2023 release:
  - Revenue rose to about $1.48 billion and operating income to about $338.8 million.
  - Management acknowledged a softer Peru market backdrop for early 2024.
  - Laureate announced a new $100 million stock repurchase authorization and framed it as returning excess capital supported by high free cash flow and a strong balance sheet.

- FY2024 release:
  - Revenue rose to about $1.57 billion and operating income to about $374.0 million.
  - Laureate said it repurchased about $102 million of stock during 2024 under the existing repurchase programs.
  - Cash was about $91.4 million and gross debt about $102.1 million at 2024-12-31, leaving net debt near $10.7 million.
  - Management stated that returning excess capital to shareholders would remain a priority for 2025.

Management-read implications:

- The 2021-2024 pattern is not acquisition-led empire building. After major portfolio simplification, capital allocation leaned toward debt cleanup, large special distributions, and later repurchases once the operating business was clearer.
- Management did acknowledge weaker Peru conditions in public releases rather than pretending both markets were equally strong.
- The biggest stewardship positive is the willingness to return excess capital instead of chasing scale through acquisitions.
- The biggest agency caution is that management language still leans heavily on adjusted EBITDA, enrollment, and growth framing, so owner orientation is visible but not pristine.
