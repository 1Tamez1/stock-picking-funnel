## Normalized Owner-Earnings View
- Conservative low case: about 230.
- Base case: about 245.
- High case: about 270.
- The base case keeps bad debt and leases fully real, uses a maintenance-capex assumption only modestly below 2025 total capex, and does not assume any heroic working-capital release.
- The key modeling sensitivity is how much of the 2025 capex increase in Peru land, new campuses, and Mexico upgrades is growth versus maintenance.

## Working-Capital And Revenue-Quality Read
- Revenue is recognized over the academic session, net of scholarships, refunds, and waivers.
- 2025 gross revenue was 2271.4 and discounts, waivers, and scholarships were 569.5, or roughly 33% of gross revenue.
- 2025 student contract receivables were 244.1 versus 189.1 in 2024.
- 2025 contract liabilities were 80.2 versus 64.3 in 2024.
- 2025 allowance for doubtful accounts was 125.1 versus 100.5 in 2024.
- The receivable and allowance build is large enough that collection quality and scholarship discipline remain core underwriting variables.

## Debt, Liquidity, And Covenant Read
- 2025 senior and other debt was 65.6 and finance lease obligations were 63.5.
- There was no revolver balance outstanding at 2025-12-31.
- Revolver size was 155.0 with maturity on 2028-09-18.
- Additional local lines of credit available were about 64.7 at 2025-12-31.
- The springing financial covenant on the revolver was not applicable at 2025-12-31 because utilization was below the threshold.
- Mexican term debt remained variable-rate and local borrowing rates were roughly 4.65% to 5.00% on certain lines and about 8.85% on the modified Mexican unsecured term loan at year-end.

## Segment And Mix Read
- 2025 revenue split: Mexico 877.4, Peru 824.4.
- 2025 direct-cost split embedded in segment disclosures still supports a high operating margin, but Peru and Mexico mix changes can move reported margins materially.
- A hypothetical 10% adverse move in average annual FX rates would have reduced 2025 revenue by about 170.2 and operating income by about 49.7.

## Financial Underwriting Takeaways
- Laureate is not funding the equity story with heavy funded debt; it ended 2025 near net cash excluding operating leases.
- The core caution is not a near-term refinancing problem. The core caution is that student receivables, allowance builds, lease obligations, and Peru durability can make reported earnings look cleaner than normalized owner economics.
- Per-share economics improved through falling diluted shares and real cash generation, but the business should still be modeled conservatively because scholarship intensity, collection quality, and FX all matter.
