Snapshot notes:

- SUNEDU's licensed-universities list says that, as of 2026-02-11, 105 universities in Peru had current institutional licenses.
- The same SUNEDU list shows Universidad Peruana de Ciencias Aplicadas (UPC) as a licensed private university in Lima/Lima with licensing date 2017-10-06.
- The same SUNEDU list shows Universidad Privada del Norte (UPN) as a licensed private university in La Libertad/Trujillo with licensing date 2017-11-11.
- SUNEDU's 2017 UPC licensing notice says UPC received institutional licensing for six years, served more than 50,000 students, had 99 programs, and operated 8 sites in Lima at the time of licensing.
- SUNEDU's 2017 UPN licensing notice says UPN received institutional licensing for six years, served more than 56,000 students, had 55 programs, and operated 9 sites, with Trujillo as its main campus plus locations in Lima and Cajamarca.
- The UPN notice also says UPN was the first private university in La Libertad to receive licensing under the SUNEDU process.

Why this matters for Business Underwriting:

- Peru's university market is regulated and licensed, which creates a real compliance barrier and screens out weaker providers, but the current licensed-university count also shows that the market is not monopolistic.
- Laureate's Peru institutions appear to have long-standing licensed status and meaningful physical scale, which supports a narrow local regulatory and trust advantage rather than a broad monopoly claim.
