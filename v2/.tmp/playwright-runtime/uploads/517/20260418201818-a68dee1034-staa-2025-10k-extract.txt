Key facts extracted for peer context:
- STAAR Surgical says it designs, develops, manufactures, and sells implantable lenses for the eye and related delivery systems.
- The company says it generates worldwide revenue almost exclusively from Implantable Collamer Lenses (ICLs).
- STAAR markets the EVO family of lenses for refractive surgery.
- For fiscal 2025, approximately 100% of STAAR net sales came from ICL sales.
- The filing says 91% of reported worldwide revenue came from product sales outside the United States.
- Search results tied to the same filing showed 2025 net sales of $239.4 million, gross profit of $182.4 million, research and development expense of about $40.1 million, and selling and marketing expense above $100 million.
- The filing also notes that historical cataract IOL products have been phased out as STAAR focused its strategy on ICL offerings.

- Peer/competitor source to help Screening distinguish RxSight's cataract-adjustable IOL model from another ophthalmic implant company focused on refractive ICLs.
